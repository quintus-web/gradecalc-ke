What is KUCCPS?

The Kenya Universities and Colleges Central Placement Service (KUCCPS) is the body that places KCSE candidates into public universities and colleges. After your results are released, KUCCPS uses your grades to calculate cluster points and match you to courses you qualify for.

📐

How Cluster Points Are Calculated

Every course has 4 specific cluster subjects. Your eligibility is based on a weighted formula:

Cluster Points = (Raw Cluster / 48) × (Aggregate / 84) × 48
  • Raw Cluster — sum of your points in the 4 cluster subjects (max 48)
  • Aggregate — sum of your best 7 subject points (max 84)
  • Final cluster points are compared against each university's cutoff
Example — Medicine
Cluster subjects: Bio (A=12) + Chem (A-=11) + Physics (B+=10) + Maths (B=9) = 42 raw
Aggregate (best 7) = 75
Cluster pts = (42/48) × (75/84) × 48 = 37.5

KCSE Subject Groups

KUCCPS organises subjects into groups. Each cluster uses subjects from specific groups.

Group I — Languages & Maths
English, Kiswahili, Mathematics
Group II — Sciences
Biology, Physics, Chemistry
Group III — Humanities
History, Geography, CRE, IRE, HRE
Group IV — Technical & Applied
Agriculture, Computer Studies, Home Science, Art & Design, Building Construction, Power Mechanics
Group V — Other Languages & Business
French, German, Arabic, Music, Business Studies

Tips for KUCCPS Application

  • Apply for at least 3 courses to increase your chances of placement
  • List courses in order of preference — KUCCPS places you in the highest you qualify for
  • Check the specific cluster subjects for each course before applying
  • Cutoffs change every year — use 2024 data as a guide, not a guarantee
  • Government-sponsored slots are limited — apply early and accurately
  • You can revise your choices during the revision window after results
